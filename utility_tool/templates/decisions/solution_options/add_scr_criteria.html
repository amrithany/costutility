Screening Criteria


Screening criteria are non-negotiable requirements that you can use to help you narrow down the list of Solution Options to a number that will be feasible to evaluate fully. You can select from the suggestions below or write in your own. 2-3 items are probably enough but use as many as are helpful.

Screening criteria should be items that can be answered yes/no quickly for each possible Solution Option.

Any criterion that cannot be assessed quickly should be entered as an "Evaluation Criterion" later in the flowchart so you can gather more information to more thoroughly evaluate whether each option meets this criterion.

You can also start with this list of requirements to prompt ideas for Solution Options.

If you don’t need to narrow the list of Solution Options, skip this step and proceed straight to the “Evaluation Criteria” step in the flowchart.

Example: If you are choosing a digital tool for middle school math practice, initial Screening Criteria might include:

  Works on Chromebooks
  Research evidence exists to show tool helps middle school students improve math performance

It may be easy to determine whether a tool works on Chromebooks but it may take more time and effort to look for or collect your own evidence of effectiveness. As a result, you may decide to move the second item to Evaluation Criteria.

Step 2:

If helpful, add details in the textboxes to the right of the Screening Criteria you selected.

Step 3:

You can write in your own Screening Criteria in the empty textboxes underneath the list of suggested items.
